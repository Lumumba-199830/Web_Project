What is a calorie?

A calorie is a unit of energy. Your body uses calories to perform every function — from breathing to exercising.

BMR

Your Basal Metabolic Rate is the number of calories your body needs at complete rest just to keep you alive.

TDEE

Your Total Daily Energy Expenditure is your BMR multiplied by your activity level. This is how many calories you burn daily.

Calorie deficit

Eating fewer calories than you burn creates a deficit, which leads to weight loss over time when done consistently.

Eat well

Making the most of your calories

It is not just about how many calories you eat — it is about the quality of those calories too.

Choose whole foods

Whole foods like vegetables, fruits, lean proteins, and whole grains keep you full longer and provide more nutrients per calorie.

Avoid empty calories

Sugary drinks, sweets, and processed snacks are high in calories but low in nutrients. Limit these where possible.

Eat regularly

Skipping meals can lead to overeating later in the day. Try to eat three balanced meals and healthy snacks if needed.
