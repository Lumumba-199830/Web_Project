Quick health advice

Simple tips for a healthier life.

Practical, easy-to-follow health tips grouped by category to help you feel your best every day.

Nutrition

What you eat directly affects how you feel, think, and perform every day.

01

Eat more vegetables

Aim to fill half your plate with vegetables at every meal. They are rich in vitamins, minerals, and fibre.

02

Cut back on sugar

Excess sugar contributes to weight gain and energy crashes. Swap sugary drinks for water or herbal tea.

03

Eat whole grains

Choose whole grain bread, rice, and pasta over refined versions. They keep you full longer and support digestion.

04

Do not skip breakfast

A nutritious breakfast jumpstarts your metabolism and helps you concentrate better throughout the morning.

05

Watch your portions

Even healthy food can cause weight gain if eaten in excess. Use smaller plates to help control portion sizes.

06

Stay hydrated

Drink at least 8 glasses of water daily. Proper hydration supports digestion, skin health, and energy levels.

Fitness

Regular physical activity is one of the best things you can do for your overall health.

01

Move every day

Even a 20-minute walk counts. Consistent daily movement is more effective than occasional intense workouts.

02

Stretch in the morning

A 5-minute morning stretch improves flexibility, reduces stiffness, and sets a positive tone for the day.

03

Take the stairs

Small choices add up. Taking the stairs instead of the lift burns more calories and strengthens your legs.

04

Strength train twice a week

Building muscle boosts your metabolism and helps maintain a healthy weight even at rest.

05

Rest between workouts

Your muscles need time to recover. Allow at least one rest day between intense training sessions.

06

Stay consistent

Consistency matters more than intensity. A simple routine done regularly will deliver better results over time.

Sleep

Quality sleep is essential for recovery, mental clarity, and long-term health.

01

Stick to a sleep schedule

Going to bed and waking up at the same time every day helps regulate your body clock and improves sleep quality.

02

Avoid screens before bed

The blue light from phones and laptops disrupts melatonin production. Put devices away at least 30 minutes before sleep.

03

Keep your room cool and dark

A cool, dark environment signals to your body that it is time to sleep and helps you stay asleep longer.

04

Limit caffeine after noon

Caffeine can stay in your system for up to 8 hours. Avoid coffee and energy drinks in the afternoon and evening.

05

Wind down before bed

A relaxing routine like reading or light stretching tells your body it is time to rest and makes falling asleep easier.

06

Aim for 7 to 9 hours

Adults need between 7 and 9 hours of sleep per night for optimal physical and mental performance.
