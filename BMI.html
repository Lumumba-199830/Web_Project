What is BMI?

Body Mass Index is a simple measurement used to assess whether a person has a healthy body weight for their height.

How it is calculated

BMI is calculated by dividing your weight in kilograms by the square of your height in metres. For example, 70kg ÷ (1.75 × 1.75) = 22.9.

Why it matters

A healthy BMI reduces your risk of heart disease, type 2 diabetes, high blood pressure, and other weight-related health conditions.

Its limitations

BMI does not measure body fat directly. Athletes may have a high BMI due to muscle mass. It is best used alongside other health checks.
